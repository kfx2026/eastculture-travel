Core Scenic Spots

Great Wall of China

Location: Huairou District, Beijing (Mutianyu Section)

Historical Background: Built over 2,000 years ago during the Warring States period, the Great Wall stretches over 13,000 miles. The Mutianyu section, constructed in the 6th century, is renowned for its strategic importance and stunning mountain views.

Highlights:

  • Less crowded than Badaling, better preserved
  • Cable car and toboggan available
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site

Opening Hours: 7:30 AM - 6:00 PM (April - October); 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM (November - March)

Tickets: ¥45 (April - October); ¥40 (November - March)

Transportation: Subway Line 2 to Dongzhimen → Bus 916 Express to Huairou → Transfer to Bus 916 Branch or taxi (approx. 2 hours total)

Best Duration: 3-4 hours

Best Season: Spring (April-May) and Autumn (September-October)

Tips: Wear comfortable shoes, bring water, arrive early to avoid crowds

Forbidden City (Palace Museum)

Location: 4 Jingshan Front Street, Dongcheng District

Historical Background: Constructed from 1406 to 1420 during the Ming Dynasty, the Forbidden City served as the imperial palace for 24 emperors. It houses over 1.8 million cultural relics.

Highlights:

  • Hall of Supreme Harmony - largest wooden structure in China
  • Imperial Garden - traditional Chinese landscaping
  • Treasure Gallery - rare imperial collections

Opening Hours: 8:30 AM - 5:00 PM (April - October); 8:30 AM - 4:30 PM (November - March). Closed on Mondays.

Tickets: ¥60 (April - October); ¥40 (November - March). Advance online booking required.

Transportation: Subway Line 1 to Tiananmen East or Tiananmen West Station → 10-minute walk

Best Duration: 3-5 hours

Best Season: All year, but avoid National Day holiday (October 1-7)

Tips: Book tickets 7 days in advance, enter from Meridian Gate (south entrance)

Featured Cuisine

Peking Duck

Flavor Profile: Crispy skin, tender meat, rich and aromatic with subtly sweet taste

Cultural Story: Dating back to the Yuan Dynasty (1271-1368), Peking Duck became a imperial dish. The technique of hanging oven roasting was perfected in the Qing Dynasty.

How to Eat: Slice duck skin and meat, wrap in thin pancake with scallion, cucumber, and sweet bean sauce

Average Price: ¥268-398 per duck (serves 2-3 people)

Zhajiangmian (Fried Sauce Noodles)

Flavor Profile: Savory, umami-rich sauce with wheat noodles, topped with fresh vegetables

Cultural Story: A beloved Beijing staple for centuries, originating from the city's working-class neighborhoods. Each family has their own recipe.

How to Eat: Mix noodles with soybean paste sauce, add cucumber strips, bean sprouts, and radish

Average Price: ¥18-35 per bowl

Folk Culture

Beijing Opera (Jingju)

Origin & History: Developed in the late 18th century, Beijing Opera combines music, vocal performance, mime, dance, and acrobatics. It was declared UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage in 2010.

Performance Elements: Four main role types (Sheng, Dan, Jing, Chou), elaborate costumes, symbolic makeup, and stylized movements

Where to Experience:Mei Lanfang Theatre, Huguang Guild Hall, Liyuan Theatre

Best Time: Evening performances (7:30 PM - 9:30 PM), book tickets in advance

Cultural Significance: Represents the essence of Chinese performing arts, storytelling through song and movement

Hutong Culture

Origin & History: Hutongs are narrow alleys formed by lines of traditional courtyard residences (Siheyuan). They date back to the Yuan Dynasty and represent old Beijing's social structure.

Experience: Walking tours, rickshaw rides, visit local courtyards, try traditional snacks

Where to Experience: Shichahai Area, Nanluoguxiang, Dongjiaomin Lane

Best Time: Morning (9:00 AM - 11:00 AM) for authentic local life

Cultural Significance: Preserves the memory of Beijing's civilian life, architecture, and community bonds

Travel Tips

4.1 Best Time to Visit

Spring (April - May): Pleasant weather, blooming flowers, ideal for outdoor attractions

Autumn (September - October): Clear skies, comfortable temperature, best season overall

Avoid: Summer (hot, humid, crowded), Winter (very cold), Chinese New Year & National Day holidays

4.2 Recommended Duration

Minimum: 3 days (Great Wall, Forbidden City, Temple of Heaven)

Ideal: 5-7 days (includes Summer Palace, Hutong tour, museums, day trips)

Extensive: 10+ days (explore suburbs, Great Wall hiking, deep cultural immersion)

4.3 Local Transportation

Subway: Extensive network (27 lines), most convenient way to travel. Purchase Yikatong card or use mobile payment (Alipay/WeChat Pay).

Bus: Cheap but slower, useful for reaching areas not covered by subway. Pay with Yikatong or mobile payment.

Taxi/DiDi: Metered taxis and ride-hailing app (DiDi). DiDi is more convenient for foreigners (has English interface).

Bike Share: Mobike, HelloBike available throughout the city. Great for short distances in central areas.

Between Attractions:

  • Forbidden City → Tiananmen Square: 5-minute walk
  • Forbidden City → Temple of Heaven: Subway Line 1 (Tiananmen East → Dongdan), transfer to Line 5 (Dongdan → Tiantandongmen), total 30 minutes
  • City Center → Great Wall (Mutianyu): Bus 916 Express + taxi, or book day tour, total 1.5-2 hours

4.4 Intercity & Airport Connections

Airport (PEK & PKX):

  • Airport Express Train: Every 15-20 minutes, 25 minutes to Dongzhimen (¥25)
  • Airport Bus: Multiple routes to different districts (¥20-30)
  • Taxi: ¥100-150 to city center (depending on traffic)

High-Speed Rail: Beijing has multiple stations (Beijing West, Beijing South, Beijing Station). Use 12306.cn app for booking.

To Other Cities:

  • Beijing → Shanghai: High-speed train (4.5 hours, ¥553-1,748)
  • Beijing → Xi'an: High-speed train (4.5 hours, ¥515.5-1,648)
  • Beijing → Chengde (for Mountain Resort): Regular train (4-6 hours, ¥41.5-86.5)

4.5 Practical Tips

  • Visa: Most foreigners need visa to enter China. Apply at Chinese embassy/consulate in advance.
  • Payments: Mobile payment (Alipay/WeChat Pay) dominates. International credit cards accepted at hotels, upscale restaurants, and some tourist attractions. Bring cash as backup.
  • Internet: Google, Facebook, WhatsApp blocked. Download VPN before arrival. Local apps: WeChat, Alipay, DiDi, 12306.
  • Language: English not widely spoken outside hotels and tourist areas. Translation apps (Google Translate, Pleco) essential.
  • Health: No vaccinations required. Tap water not drinkable, buy bottled water. Air quality varies, check AQI before outdoor activities.
  • Safety: Beijing is very safe. Watch for pickpockets in crowded areas. Avoid demonstrations and political gatherings.
  • Etiquette: No tipping culture. Remove shoes when entering someone's home. Don't stick chopsticks vertically in rice (resembles incense for the dead).
